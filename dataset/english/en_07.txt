Economy and Global Markets
Economy
The global economy is an interconnected system of production, trade, investment, and consumption that spans every country on Earth. Markets coordinate the decisions of billions of individuals, businesses, and governments, allocating resources and determining prices through the interplay of supply and demand. Economic growth, measured by changes in gross domestic product, reflects increases in the total value of goods and services produced by a society.

International trade allows countries to specialize in the goods and services they produce most efficiently and to exchange these for products they cannot produce as well themselves. Major trading blocs and agreements, such as the World Trade Organization, shape the rules of global commerce. Financial markets, including stock exchanges and currency markets, allow investors to allocate capital to productive uses across borders and time horizons.

Banks and financial institutions play a central role in the economy, channeling savings into investment and providing credit to businesses and households. Sound monetary policy, conducted by central banks, helps maintain price stability and sustainable economic growth. Government fiscal policy — decisions about taxation and public spending — affects the distribution of resources and the pace of economic activity.

Economic development aims to improve living standards and reduce poverty by expanding productive capacity, creating jobs, and improving the distribution of income. Investments in education, infrastructure, healthcare, and technology are key drivers of long-term economic growth. Business innovation and entrepreneurship create new products and services that expand the economic frontier and improve human welfare.
