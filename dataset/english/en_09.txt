Culture, Arts, and Human Heritage
Culture
Culture encompasses the shared practices, values, beliefs, language, art, and traditions that define a community and connect its members across generations. Human cultures are extraordinarily diverse, reflecting the different environments, histories, and choices of societies around the world. This diversity is itself a form of wealth, offering a vast repertoire of solutions to the challenges of human existence.

Music is perhaps the most universal form of human expression, found in every culture throughout history. From traditional folk songs to classical symphonies and contemporary popular music, melody and rhythm provide a medium for emotion, identity, and social bonding. Literature — in all its forms, from oral storytelling to the novel — preserves human experience and transmits values, wisdom, and imagination across time and space.

Visual arts, including painting, sculpture, photography, and film, give form to human experience and perception. Architecture shapes the built environment and expresses the aspirations and values of societies. Dance, theater, and performance arts bring communities together in shared moments of meaning and joy. Craft traditions — weaving, pottery, woodworking — embody accumulated knowledge and aesthetic sensibilities passed down through generations.

Cultural heritage, both tangible and intangible, connects communities to their pasts and provides a foundation for identity and belonging. Preserving languages, traditions, and historical sites is essential for maintaining the diversity of human culture. At the same time, cultures are always changing, absorbing new influences and generating new forms of expression that reflect contemporary experience.
